INTERACTION DESIGN
FOR THE WEB/ SEMANTIC
MARKUP

Week 3

User Alignment:

  • I want people who view my website to look at the way I structure my pages and the colour schemes I use and see it as familiar. But, I also want them to
    be impressed at the little breaks of convention that I will use to make my website creative and unique. I want my users to explore every single page,
    hyperlink, and tab that I use on my website. This is so that they have the full experience and they see all the work that I put into it.


  • My target audience, for now, is our lecturer for this course. I know that she has access to a computer and has experience in web development so she
    will be able to view my website and when some things don't pan out the way I wanted them to, she might be able to figure out what went wrong.
    Eventually, my target audience will be potential employers in the industry of digital art and technology. This will be my new target audience because
    when applying for jobs, I will need to submit my CV and portfolio so they would need to see my website. This means that my website will be
    modified, checked for errors, and made ready to submit for applications and interviews.


  • I assume that potential employers won't have a lot of time on their hands so the website has to be easy to navigate, functional, and aesthetically
    pleasing. This is because when the website is easy to navigate, it won't take a lot of time to view it, when it is functional, they won't have any issues or
    have to send me emails requesting a functional website, and when it is aesthetically pleasing they will see that I am creative and I organise things
    nicely and neatly. Potential employers, I also assume, want to see who I am as an individual and how I differ from other applicants so I will make sure
    that my profile page is very detailed and that my portfolio page is a representation of my best work.

My site's content, experience, and interface elements:

How does the exercise above relate to my structure, URL scheme, and semantic markup?

  • This exercise helped me redefine the structure of my website. Instead of just putting things where I think they should be, I started looking at how a
    user might view this. Allocating the content of my website to the important, least important, and interesting categories showed me which pages I
    should put most of my focus on. For instance, the Homepage, Blog, and Design are the most important because the first is what the user sees first,
    and the other two are what I will be marked most on according to the rubric. My URL scheme is "http" and that means it is not secure. I am not 100%
    sure but I think this means that the site is vulnerable to hacking or external alteration. This means that further along in my career I would have to get
    an SSL (secure sockets layer) certificate so that my website is secure and that it can be published on Google.


  • Finally, in terms of semantic markup, this exercise taught me that all my HTML files should have the same structure just with different content. What
    I mean by this is that all my HTML files will have the same metadata information, they will have headers, footers, nav tags, main tags, and heading
    tags. Then within the main tags, the information will be different. This is so that everything is the same throughout and if there is a problem I can fix
    it for all of them instead of trying to find out where it is. I will not use div and span tags unless necessary and I will try my best to use tags for what
    they can do.

Reflection on my development progress for the week.

  • This week I learnt a lot about web development. I always thought it was about code and seeing your work on a website but it is deeper than that. It is
    about what you want to do, what you want your users to see, and how you make them do what you want them to do because "we are in the business
    of manipulating people" (Geyser, H). In terms of the actual development of my website, this week I added metadata to each of my HTML files to
    describe the content of each page. I also added my wireframes from last week to my Design page. The content of my Blog and Design pages is
    structured inside tables. This is because the table creates a nice layout for my page and it doesn't like I'm looking at a bunch of paragraphs on the
    screen. Apart from this, I also started putting together all my drawings, artworks, and games that will be displayed on my Portfolio page. I know that
    they are not necessary for now but I just want to have everything in one place so that when I have learnt enough skills I can start populating those
    pages instead of being told to do so.

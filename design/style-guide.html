STYLE GUIDE

My goal and aesthetic

I want my website to look bright but not in a glaring way hence I will be using pastel colours. The background, navigation tabs, and next button colours will be consistent on every page. I also want it to feel friendly but also professional therfore my buttons will be a mixture between straight and rounded edges. This realtes to my content because it is both professional and also playful and creative. The blog, design page, and essays will be assessed for the course but the profile and portfolio are my way of showing who I am and the projects I have done, which will be "assessed" by a potential employer.

Colour Palette & Font Style

This is the first colour palatte that I might use for my website. I got this image from the internet and I chose it because it has hex codes which will be useful when I add styling to my website. Drafts for the UI Overview

Text Alignment

  1. As seen in the UI overview, I want my website name to appear at the top left of all my pages besides the homepage. This is just a way to put the title on the left so that the user focuses on the other parts of the page. The headings on my all pages will also be alighned to the left.
  2. My homepage's website name will be aligned at the center of the page. Because this alignment is not recommended for longer text, I want to use it this once for the opening page of my website. Also, I have seen a lot of websites that use centre alignment in this way that I found visually appealing, so I'm hoping it will be the same for mine.
  3. All the content in my webpages will have justified alignment. This is because it makes the work look neat and easy to read.
  4. Finally, as seen in the UI overview, my navigation menu will appear at the top right of all my pages besides the homepage. This is so that it doesn't collide with the website name and it also makes the page look visually appealing.
